# Data Science Portfolio — Analytical Foundations and Applied Machine Learning

This repository provides a curated overview of my data science portfolio, developed as part of a deliberate transition into **data analysis and data science** following an established professional career grounded in complex analytical work.

The projects collected here emphasise **analytical judgement, structured reasoning, and clarity of communication**. They reflect a disciplined approach to problem framing, explicit documentation of assumptions, and careful interpretation of results.

Alongside this foundation, I am actively extending my capability into supervised and unsupervised machine learning, neural networks, and applied modelling. Rather than treating machine learning as a purely technical exercise, I approach it as a natural extension of analytical thinking — focused on understanding behaviour, evaluating trade-offs, and aligning outputs with real-world decision-making.

Together, these projects demonstrate how I approach analytical problems: thoughtfully, transparently, and with an awareness that technical work must ultimately serve organisational context and human judgement.

## How I work

Across projects, I prioritise:

- **Analytical judgement**  
  Understanding whether a method is appropriate before applying it.
- **Documentation and clarity**  
  Making reasoning, assumptions, and limitations explicit.
- **Interpretability**  
  Explaining *why* results appear, not just *what* they are.
- **Real-world context**  
  Considering how outputs would be used by stakeholders.

My machine learning skills are developing iteratively, but they are grounded in a broader analytical mindset shaped by experience, communication, and problem framing.
